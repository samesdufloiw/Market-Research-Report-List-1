<p><p>Axial bone densitometry focuses on assessing bone density, mainly in the spine and hips, to evaluate fracture risk. Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (DEXA) is the most widely used method, employing low-dose X-rays to measure bone mineral density. Quantitative Computed Tomography (QCT) offers a 3D assessment by evaluating volumetric bone density, providing precise insights into bone strength. Both methods are crucial for diagnosing osteoporosis and guiding treatment, each offering unique advantages in patient evaluation and management.</p></p>
