<p><p>Dysphagia management encompasses therapeutic and technological solutions for swallowing difficulties, which can be categorized into high (oropharyngeal) and low (esophageal) dysphagia markets. High dysphagia involves challenges in initiating swallowing, often treated with muscle exercises or assistive devices. Conversely, low dysphagia relates to issues in the esophagus, requiring interventions such as dilation techniques or medications. Both markets focus on improving patients' quality of life through tailored strategies, addressing the distinct physiological mechanisms involved in each type of dysphagia.</p></p>
