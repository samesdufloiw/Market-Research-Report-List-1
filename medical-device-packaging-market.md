<p><p>The medical device packaging market comprises several key types, including trays, pouches, clamshells, and others. Pouches are flexible packaging options often used for smaller devices, allowing for easy storage and access. Clamshells offer a transparent, protective shell that securely houses devices while enabling visibility. <p><p>The medical device packaging market includes two primary applications: sterile and non-sterile packaging. Sterile packaging is crucial for maintaining the integrity and safety of medical devices that require a contamination-free environment, often utilizing techniques like steam or ethylene oxide sterilization. Non-sterile packaging, on the other hand, is employed for devices that are safe to use without sterilization, focusing on protection during transportation and storage. Both packaging types are essential for ensuring product efficacy and compliance with regulatory standards.</p></p>
