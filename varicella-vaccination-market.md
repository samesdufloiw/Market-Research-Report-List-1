<p><p>The Varicella vaccination market is categorized into two main types: monovalent and combination vaccines. Monovalent vaccines specifically target the varicella virus, providing immunity against chickenpox alone. In contrast, combination vaccines include multiple antigens, such as measles, mumps, rubella, and varicella (MMRV), offering protection against several diseases in a single shot. Both types play a crucial role in preventing varicella infections, with monovalent vaccines typically recommended for children and combination vaccines providing a convenient option for broader immunization.</p></p>
