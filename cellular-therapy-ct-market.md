<p><p>Cellular therapy (CT) refers to treatments that involve the administration of live cells to restore or enhance tissue function. The market is divided into two main types: stem cell and non-stem cell therapies. Stem cell therapies utilize pluripotent or multipotent cells to regenerate damaged tissues and treat various conditions, such as cancers and degenerative diseases. Non-stem cell therapies, on the other hand, involve the use of differentiated cells, like immune cells or genetically modified cells, to target specific diseases, including autoimmune disorders and certain cancers.</p></p>
